About

My work as a pastoral counselor is grounded in a belief that we are formed in relationship - and that healing also happens in relationship.

I am trained in the Mannock Integrated Method (MIM), an approach that integrates psychology, neurobiology, and theology. This framework allows me to attend not only to what you are experiencing, but to how your story has been shaped over time - in relationships, in your body, and in your emotional world.

I am less interested in offering quick solutions, and more interested in understanding your experience with you. Many people come to counseling carrying things they have had to navigate alone. My hope is to offer a space where those experiences can be seen, named, and held differently.

The work we do together may involve slowing down, noticing patterns, and making sense of how your past continues to shape your present. Over time, this can lead to a greater sense of clarity, integration, and freedom in how you move through your life and relationships.

I hold a posture of curiosity, care, and respect for your story. You do not need to have everything figured out before beginning.
